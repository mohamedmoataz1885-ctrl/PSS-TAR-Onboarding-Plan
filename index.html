Job Description

Retubing and Inspection for the Compander Compressor Aftercooler 1E-1533

Discipline

Static Equipment

Asset

Compander Compressor Aftercooler (1E-1533)

Est. Duration

25 Shifts

➢ Summary:
Start by isolating the exchanger, removing the spools connected to the channel heads, then dismantling and lifting the channel heads to access the tube bundle.

From TAR 22, 127 tubes were retubed out of 366; in this turnaround, the scope is to verify the wall thickness/integrity of those previously installed 127 tubes by cleaning using hydrojetting machine at 1350 barg (same pressure used in TAR 22) followed by IRIS inspection (Internal Rotary Inspection System) for all 127 tubes.

For the remaining 239 tubes, perform full retubing by cutting tubes behind the tubesheet, removing the tube-to-tubesheet weld, pulling out the tubes, then beveling and installing new tubes, followed by inspection, welding, and expanding the tubes to the tubesheet. Perform shell-side pneumatic leak test (2 barg, 1-hr hold) and shell-side hydrotest at 20 barg and hold for 30-min to confirm all tubes are properly installed, then reassemble/reinstate.

➢ Trigger:
Medium RBI (Risk Based Inspection) Risk rating.
Historical metal loss and potential tube leaks.

➢ Value:
Confirm asset integrity and ensure safe operation.

Retube the remaining tubes (239 tubes)

  • 4.1. Cut tubes behind the tubesheet from both ends.
  • 4.2. Remove the tube-to-tubesheet weld.
  • 4.3. Pull out the tubes (from the south side).
  • 4.4. Remove the studs from the north side.

  • 4.7. Tack weld the tubes from one end.
  • 4.8. Remove the tube excess from the other end using the tube end face tool.
  • 4.9. Weld the tubes to the tubesheet as per the repair plan ITP (Inspection and Test Plan).
  • 4.10. Perform PT inspection to the welded joints.
TAR
5

Inspection after retubing

  • 5.1. Install blinds and connect air hoses to the air grid for pneumatic test (Regulator to control the pressure is required).
  • 5.2. Rise the pressure gradually until we reach 2 barg. Hold for 1 hr.
  • 5.3. Confirm there is no leaks from tube-to-tubesheet welds, tubes using soap.
  • 5.4. Release the air pressure slowly.
  • 5.5. Expand tubes against the tubesheet using the expander tool.
  • 5.6. Connected water hoses to the hydrojet machine for hydrotest.
  • 5.7. Rise the pressure gradually until we reach 20 barg. Hold for 30 min.
  • 5.8. Confirm there is no leaks from tube-to-tubesheet welds, tubes.
  • 5.9. Release the hydrotest pressure and drain the test water.
TAR
6

Reassembly & Boxing-up

  • 6.1.Reinstall channel heads and reconnect spools.
  • 6.2.Remove blinds and handover to operations.

Job Specific Hazards

1

Trapped pressure / sudden release during line breaking

Gas or liquid under pressure

Main Controls

  • Ensure isolation certificate is in place
  • Prove isolation by operator
  • Confirm isolation by PA
  • Break joints slowly
2

High pressure hydro-jetting

Hose whip / loss of control

Main Controls

  • Conduct a low-RPM test run, ramp up gradually, and check for leaks
  • Stop immediately and replace any leaking hose/connection
  • Fit whipchecks at each connection (ensure correct pressure rating)
  • Only trained/competent personnel to do the job
  • Depressurize/isolate hoses when not in use/unattended
  • Barricade the area; allow authorized personnel with correct PPE only
  • Ensure all hoses/fittings are rated above the pump max pressure
3

Diesel equipment

Fire / spill / exhaust affecting nearby ventilation

Main Controls

  • Provide spill containment under equipment
  • Keep a fire extinguisher adjacent
  • Complete a daily equipment checklist by a competent person and follow MSDS
  • Assign a person to monitor during operation & ensure everyone knows the stop button
  • Keep exhaust away from ventilation of nearby confined spaces
4

Hot work (welding / grinding / cutting)

Fire / explosion & fumes

Main Controls

  • Remove flammables and perform gas test before start
  • Maintain continuous gas monitoring
  • Assign fire watch with correct extinguisher (continue 30 mins after completion)
  • Use welding blankets
  • Ensure competent personnel equipped with correct PPE (face shield/respirator)
5

Pressurized systems

Gas cylinders / hoses under pressure (hose whip / sudden release)

Main Controls

  • Secure cylinders upright (chained, ventilated, away from heat)
  • Close cylinder valves and depressurize hoses during breaks/unattended
  • Conduct soap leak test when opening argon cylinders
  • Use inspected cylinders only
6

Hydrotest pressurization hazards

Stored pressure / line-of-fire / overpressure from weak links

Main Controls

  • Barricade the hydrotest zone and notify all parties before pressurization
  • Use only rated/inspected hoses and temporary fittings, correctly routed
  • Remove non-rated instruments and confirm MAWP (Maximum Allowable Working Pressure) rating before testing
  • Depressurize/isolate hoses when unattended
  • After hydrotest, confirm gauge reads zero and break joints slowly
  • Wear face shield and required PPE within the test area
